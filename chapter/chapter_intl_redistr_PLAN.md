## Outline

1. Introduction: 
- why international redistribution: sustainability is also about global justice; 1% of HICs GDP represents LICs GDP; int'l redistribution of resources and power necessary to fight poverty rapidly (Fabre JDS 2025), also justified by historical responsibility of HICs (colonization, climate debt...) and utilitarianism (Kopczuk et al)
- the low extent of int'l redistr: explain that most of it is through foreign aid; compare flows of foreign aid, remittances, and profits evaded from Global South
- why not more redistr? One key element, analyzed here, is attitudes of the public in HICs: does intl' redistr align with people values (or do they defend their monetary interest)? do they trust institutions like foreign aid? do they support international taxation, climate policy or global democracy? are they willing to contribute themselves? as we'll show, survey results show agreement with int'l redistr. We'll then discuss why these proposals lack prominence. One possibility would be unreliability of stated preferences: attitudes robust in many survey experiments, framings and contexts. In last section, we argue lack of prominence is due to low salience, itself fueled by pluralistic ignorance, existing national institutions (vote, media, legal notion of sovereignty), and that other issues dominate voting choices.
